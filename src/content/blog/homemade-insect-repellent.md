With the summer season approaching, many of our clients in the cosmetic and wellness industry are looking for natural alternatives to synthetic bug sprays. Creating a homemade insect repellent is not only simple but also allows you to control the quality of the ingredients you use.

### The Power of Nature's Repellents

Many essential oils are renowned for their natural insect-repelling properties. Based on our research and years of supply chain experience, here are some of the most effective oils for specific pests:

* **Mosquitoes:** [Citronella](/products/citronella-oil), [Peppermint](/products/peppermint-oil), [Lemon Eucalyptus](/products/lemon-eucalyptus-oil), [Lemongrass](/products/lemongrass-oil), [Lavender](/products/lavender-essential-oil), and [Eucalyptus](/products/eucalyptus-oil).
* **Fleas:** [Cedarwood](/products/cedarwood-oil), [Tea Tree](/products/tea-tree-oil), and [Lavender](/products/lavender-essential-oil).
* **Ticks:** Rose Geranium, Thyme, and Grapefruit.

### The DIY Recipe

To make your own repellent, use a 4 oz clean spray bottle and follow this simple formula:

1.  **Water:** Fill with 2 ounces of distilled or boiled water.
2.  **Base:** Add 1 ounce of witch hazel or vodka.
3.  **Essential Oils:** Add 50 to 75 drops of your chosen essential oils. (Keep the total percentage of essential oils under 15% for safety).
4.  **Bonus:** Add approximately 0.5 ounces of Jojoba oil if you want moisturizing properties.

**Pro Tip:** Don't fill the bottle to the very top—you need room to shake it before each use! Because essential oils and water do not naturally mix, they will separate, so a good shake is essential before every spray.

### Recommended Blend for Beginners
If you are just starting, I recommend a blend of **Citronella, Tea Tree, and Peppermint**. If you have it on hand, definitely add **Lavender** for a refreshing scent or **Lemon Eucalyptus**, which several studies have shown to be the most effective of all natural counterparts.
