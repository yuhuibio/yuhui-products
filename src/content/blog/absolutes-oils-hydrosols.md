In the world of natural skincare ingredients, understanding production methods is key to choosing the right raw materials for your formulations. While terms like "absolutes," "essential oils," and "hydrosols" are often used interchangeably, they represent distinct extraction processes, chemical profiles, and applications.

## 1. Essential Oils: The Gold Standard
[Essential oils](https://www.yuhuibiotec.com/products) are primarily produced via steam distillation. Boiling water creates steam that flows through raw plant material, stripping away aromatic chemical compounds. As the steam cools, it condenses, and the oil that floats to the surface is collected. 

*   **Key Characteristic**: They capture the volatile aromatic compounds of the plant.
*   **Best For**: Aromatherapy, therapeutic skincare, and natural healing.

## 2. Absolutes: The Perfumer's Choice
Absolutes are highly concentrated, potent aromatic substances. They are created by soaking delicate plant material (such as Jasmine or Rose) in solvents like hexane. Once the solvent evaporates, the fragrant wax-like absolute remains. 

*   **Key Characteristic**: Because no heat is used, absolutes capture a more complex, "true-to-life" scent profile than distillation can offer.
*   **Best For**: High-end perfumery and luxury cosmetic scenting.

## 3. Hydrosols: Gentle and Soothing
Hydrosols, often called floral waters, are the aromatic water that remains after the essential oil is separated from the condensed steam. While they contain a lower concentration of fragrant molecules, they are infused with water-soluble plant properties.

*   **Key Characteristic**: Gentle, hydrating, and typically pH-balanced.
*   **Best For**: Facial toners, refreshing body mists, and formulations for sensitive skin.
