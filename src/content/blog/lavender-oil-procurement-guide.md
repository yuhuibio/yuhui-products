### The "Hidden" Variety Problem
The lavender fields in Xinjiang are renowned, yet a common issue plagues the supply chain: the blending of different varieties during extraction. While the premium *French Blue* variety is known for its superior profile, the *Lavandula latifolia* variety is also cultivated extensively.

When these varieties are processed together, it leads to inconsistent scent profiles and chemical compositions. For a professional buyer, this lack of distinction is a major challenge in maintaining product quality.

### French Blue vs. Lavandula latifolia: The Scientific Distinction
To help our clients make informed procurement decisions, we have analyzed data from comparative trials, including findings published by Hunan Agricultural University. This data underscores the critical differences between these varieties:

| Feature | French Blue (Lavandula angustifolia) | Lavandula latifolia |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Extraction Source** | Pure flower heads | Flower heads, stalks, and leaves |
| **Scent Profile** | Pure, rich floral notes | Herbaceous, slight spicy undertones |
| **Chemical Focus** | High in Esters (gentle) | Higher in Eucalyptol (mildly stimulating) |
| **Oil Yield** | 5% – 6% | Up to 8% (combined) |
| **Best Application** | Luxury perfumery, skincare | Industrial functional products |

*Data reference: Based on comparative testing and research data from Hunan Agricultural University.*
